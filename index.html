About Me

Welcome! I am Thresa Kelly. I am a Ph.D. student in Astrophysical Sciences and Technology (AST) at the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT). My research interests include active galactic nuclei (AGN), black holes, and galaxy evolution.

Currently, I am working with Dr. Andrew Robinson and Dr. Jeyhan Kartaltepe at RIT. The goal of this research is to estimate black hole masses for AGN in the COSMOS field. This work is funded by a NSF GRFP fellowship beginning in Fall 2024.

I graduated from the University of Kansas in 2023 with a B.S. Engineering Physics: Digital Electronic Systems and a minor in Astronomy. I worked on AGN research with Dr. Allison Kirkpatrick. I also attended an REU program at the University of Hawaii Institute for Astronomy in 2022, and was mentored By Dr. David Sanders and Connor Auge. My project was Accretion History of AGN: A Multiwavelength Comparison of X-ray and Infrared Selected Active Galactic Nuclei.
